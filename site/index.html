What this website is about

A practical demonstration of how agentic AI can support NHS business and performance work — reporting, analysis, mandatory reporting assurance, admin-heavy tasks and subject matter support — when properly governed and paired with human accountability.

AI can help draft, structure, analyse, check and explain performance intelligence, but accountable humans remain responsible for definitions, validation, judgement and sign-off.
Demonstration caveat: This is a personal demonstration site. It is not an official Dorset HealthCare website or report, is not operationally validated, and does not contain confidential, unpublished internal or patient-identifiable information.

Purpose of this site

This microsite supports my application for the Business & Performance Business Partner role. It shows how I would approach performance intelligence, mandatory reporting assurance, and responsible use of agentic AI — not as a replacement for analysts or subject matter experts, but as structured support for work that is often admin-heavy, specification-heavy and time-pressured.

Each page demonstrates a different capability: a mandatory reporting overview, draft performance reports, an agent operating model, and governance controls. Nothing here is a live Trust system.

How to read this site: Start here, then visit the four pages in order using the menu above or the links at the foot of this page. Each page opens with a short plain-English summary, so you do not need a data or technology background to follow it.

How this was built

The site was built with support from a Cursor agent, using prompts and human direction at every stage. I specified the structure, content, governance boundaries and tone; the agent helped draft HTML, R scripts and agent rule documents. I reviewed and directed changes throughout.

  • Static HTML, CSS and lightweight JavaScript — no backend or database
  • Reproducible R scripts in R/ for synthetic data and report generation
  • A separate public-data/ folder cataloguing publicly available NHS aggregate sources
  • No connection to live Trust systems

Data scope

All performance reports on this site use synthetic aggregate data — fabricated figures for demonstration only. Where public NHS sources are referenced, links point to published national statistics or specifications. No patient-identifiable data, confidential management information or unpublished internal documents are included.

Public source metadata is catalogued in public-data/DATA_SOURCE_REGISTER.csv for transparency about what could be used in a governed production workflow.

What agentic AI can support here

  • Reporting drafts — first-draft narrative, structure and commentary from approved or synthetic data
  • Analysis structure — organising trends, caveats and questions for human review
  • Mandatory reporting assurance — identifying, organising and maintaining reporting requirements from public specifications
  • Admin-heavy coordination — project tracking, backlog alignment, developer activity logging (demonstrated conceptually)
  • Subject matter support — answering definition questions from approved sources with citations, so the user can check at source

Agentic AI can support, structure and accelerate this work when properly governed. It does not make complex performance management straightforward — it helps teams spend more time on judgement, stakeholder work and improvement.

What AI does not do

  • Replace subject matter experts, information leads or clinical judgement
  • Make operational or service decisions
  • Approve reports for services, directors or Board
  • Submit mandatory returns without human validation
  • Use confidential, unpublished or patient-identifiable data without explicit Information Governance (IG) approval — the rules that protect how NHS data is used

Why this is relevant to the Business & Performance Business Partner role

The Business & Performance Business Partner sits between services, data, reporting, assurance, improvement and decision-making. That role needs visibility of mandatory returns, credible interpretation of performance data, and the ability to turn analysis into practical action — while developing performance frameworks, presenting complex information clearly to services and senior leaders, and leading a small team.

This site reflects that work through examples rather than claims. It shows how governed agentic AI could help a business partner and their team move faster on first drafts and assurance, without weakening accountability for definitions, validation or sign-off.
