Demo Rivers Health (DRH) — Jan to Mar 2026 focus

Synthetic demonstration only. Fictional Demo Rivers Health (DRH) warehouse outputs. Not Dorset HealthCare (RDY) public data. Not official reporting. Human sign-off required.

Question

How does the mock warehouse support investigation of urgent-care activity movements in early 2026 — distinguishing genuine operational pressure from extract or attribution artefacts?

March 2026 — CareCase spike without IUCS corroboration

Cases up, activity down — extract change aligns

Field Reading
FigureCases opened (all): 542 (+3% MoM). Operational cases (demo filter): 197. IUCS contacts: 1,272 (−14% MoM). Cases without SourceContactId: 322 of 542.
ComparatorFeb 2026: 525 cases, 402 operational, 1,477 IUCS, 123 without source contact.
TrendDiverging — case opens and IUCS moving in opposite directions; missing-source cases sharply up.
JudgementLikely artefactual — needs review — extract change log records CareCase inclusion rule change effective March run (2026-02-28).
Evidencevolume_trends.csv; DQ002; source_profiling_report.md §5; localops_extract_change_log.xlsx (CareCase, NightlyCaseExtract).

Bottom line

Early 2026 shows mixed signals. Jan–Feb uplift is corroborated across multiple synthetic sources and may reflect genuine operational pressure. March case increase is not supported by IUCS contacts and aligns with a CareCase extract rule change — treat as extract-driven until confirmed. December date-boundary flags need export-schedule review before month attribution. Do not blend these DRH warehouse findings with public RDY briefs.
