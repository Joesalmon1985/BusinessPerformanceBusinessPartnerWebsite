Three principles

An expedition operating model for performance partnering during system change — disciplined, empirical, and alert.

What I hope to achieve

Consistent KPI definitions, reporting rhythm, validation checks, clear ownership, a standard route from issue to action, and documentation good enough for handover.

My approach to succeed

Apply Fanatic Discipline, Empirical Creativity and Productive Paranoia daily — not as slogans, but as practical habits that protect reporting meaning during migration.

Expedition principles applied to performance

1. Fanatic Discipline — the 10-mile march

March 10 miles every day. Steady progress under pressure — not 30 miles when easy and zero when hard.

  • Consistent KPI definitions and reporting rhythm
  • One agreed definition chain, used consistently across mandatory and local reporting
  • Standard route: issue → analysis → action → review
  • Documentation for handover

The risk is not just data migration. It is definition migration.

Definition migration example

2. Empirical Creativity — test the ice

Test uncertain ground with scouts and small routes before moving the whole expedition.

  • Prototype before scaling
  • Test definition chains against known patients before embedding in warehouse
  • Run old and new outputs side by side

I would not treat a new data model as automatically meaning the same thing in a different format. I would test whether it still answers the service question.

Three options tested

3. Productive Paranoia — pack for the storm

Plan fallbacks while the sky is clear. Stay alert when familiar or unfamiliar.

  • Must-not-fail reports and fallback routes
  • Reporting confidence marking when the chain may have drifted
  • Assume reports can look stable while definition drift makes them unsafe

The dangerous point in migration is when the dashboard still refreshes, but the measure no longer means what people think it means.
