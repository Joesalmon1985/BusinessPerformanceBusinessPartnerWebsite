Worked example: AI-assisted MHSDS public-data briefing

MHSDS six-month performance brief — Nov 2025 to Apr 2026

Public-data demonstration report: This is not an official Dorset HealthCare report. It uses public aggregate data only and requires human review and local owner confirmation before operational use.

What the agent was asked to do

Business question: From six months of public MHSDS Provider/RDY data, summarise access and activity measures, describe descriptive trends, and list what a mental health data owner must confirm.

Dataset: MHSDS Monthly Statistics — stacked Provider/RDY trend file

This is a first-draft analytical brief for human review — not an approved performance report.

Data used

  • Primary: trend_mhsds_access_rdy.csv — MHS23, MHS01, MHS29, MHS69 (Provider/RDY)
  • Context: demo_mhsds_activity.csv — latest-month slice for suppression audit
  • Fallback reference: rdy_mhsds_monthly_mhsds_monthly_performance-april-2026_time_series_MHSDS Time_Series_data_Apr_2016_MarPrf_2023.csv
  • Period covered: Nov 2025 – Apr 2026 (6 months, provisional MHSDS, Provider/RDY)
  • RDY filter: ODS code RDY; Dorset HealthCare trust name variants (see filter notes)
  • Historic trend data: Yes — six-month stacked trend (6 months per measure from trend_mhsds_access_rdy.csv)

What this report can and cannot tell us

This report can:

  • Summarise six months of key MHSDS access and activity measures for RDY as provider.
  • Show latest vs previous month and six-month descriptive change.
  • Flag suppression, provisional data and breakdown scope limits.

This report cannot:

  • Support operational access conclusions without local MHSDS validation.
  • Combine provider and ICB-resident breakdowns into one headline.
  • Explain why trends changed or infer access improvement from contact counts alone.

For that reason, this should be treated as a first-draft prompt for review, not a final performance judgement.

Headline reading

  • Six-month public MHSDS brief for Dorset HealthCare (RDY as provider), covering Nov 2025 – Apr 2026 (6 months, provisional MHSDS, Provider/RDY).
  • Measures with a rising pattern over the window: MHS23, MHS01.
  • Measures broadly stable over six months: MHS29.
  • Volatile movement (no clear direction): MHS69.
  • Open referrals (MHS23) are a stock measure — not automatically good or bad. Contacts (MHS29) are activity volume — not proof of access improvement.
  • Provider and ICB-resident breakdowns must not be summed into one trust-wide headline.

Caveats (read before interpreting)
  • MHSDS monthly data are provisional and may revise on final refresh.
  • Suppressed values (*) are not treated as zero and are excluded from averages and charts.
  • Figures are Provider/RDY rows only — not ICB-resident population views.
  • Stock measures (open referrals, in-contact counts) and activity measures (contacts) must not be read as access performance without local validation.
  • Latest-month demo extract contains 77 suppressed cells across all measures — see audit trail.

Human validation checklist

  • Is this the same definition used locally? — Public datasets may use national definitions. Local dashboards may use different filters, denominators or reporting dates.
  • Is this still the latest position? — Public data may be delayed. Local operational data may have moved on since the reporting period shown.
  • Is the figure affected by data quality, suppression, coding or service model? — A public figure can be affected by small numbers, exclusions, coding rules or how services are configured.
  • Who owns the local narrative? — The accountable service, finance, workforce, quality or BI owner should confirm the explanation before use.
  • Is this suitable for board/service reporting, or only a prompt for further review? — These briefs are first drafts — confirm whether the figure is ready for formal reporting or needs more local work.
  • Does the local MHSDS definition match these public Provider/RDY measures? — Confirm MHS23 open referrals and MHS01 in-contact definitions with the data owner.
  • Are suppressed or missing months material for your reporting? — Check value_status in trend file — MHS23: 0 suppressed; MHS01: 0 suppressed; MHS29: 0 suppressed; MHS69: 0 suppressed.
  • Should Provider or ICB-resident breakdowns be used for this question? — This brief uses Provider/RDY only — do not sum with resident breakdowns.
