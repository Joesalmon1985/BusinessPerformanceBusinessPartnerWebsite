NHS Oversight Framework — RDY first-draft brief (Q4 2025/26)

Public-data demonstration report: This is not an official Dorset HealthCare report. It uses public aggregate data only and requires human review and local owner confirmation before operational use.

What the agent was asked to do

Business question: Using the latest public NHS Oversight Framework file for mental health and community trusts, prepare a first-draft RDY performance brief: which raw metrics exist, what do published median/rank fields show, and what must a human verify?

Dataset: NHS Oversight Framework MH/community CSV (RDY)

This is a first-draft analytical brief for human review — not an approved performance report.

Data used

  • NHS Oversight Framework (demo_nof_overview.csv + full RDY processed extract)
  • trend_nof_rdy.csv — quarter-over-quarter headline metrics
  • Assurance index (demo_assurance_profile.csv) where available
  • Period covered: Q4 2025/26 snapshot; quarter-over-quarter trend Jul 2025 – Jan 2026
  • RDY filter: ODS code RDY; Dorset HealthCare trust name variants (see filter notes)
  • Historic trend data: Yes — quarter-over-quarter Value trends (Jul 2025 – Jan 2026 from trend_nof_rdy.csv); median/rank latest quarter only

What this report can and cannot tell us

This report can:

  • Show where RDY appears to sit against published peer median and rank fields.
  • Help identify areas that may need local review.

This report cannot:

  • Explain why a metric is high or low.
  • Confirm whether the public figure matches the latest internal position, local definitions or operational context.

For that reason, this should be treated as a first-draft prompt for review, not a final performance judgement.

Headline reading

  • The public NOF snapshot includes 11 raw RDY metrics across 5 domains.
  • The strongest-looking published ranks are for community waits over 52 weeks and planned surplus/deficit — both still need definition checks before being described as positive performance.
  • The main areas flagged for local review are urgent community response 2-hour performance, mental health crisis face-to-face contact within 24 hours, adult mental health inpatients with length of stay over 60 days, and relative difference in costs.
  • Finance metrics need specific finance-owner interpretation because the public fields do not explain the local plan, sign convention or internal reporting context.
  • Quarter-over-quarter trends for headline metrics (Jul 2025 – Jan 2026) are available from trend_nof_rdy.csv — descriptive Value movement only; median/rank remain latest-quarter snapshots.

Key findings by review area

Potential strengths, subject to validation

Percentage of patients waiting over 52 weeks for community services.

RDY is shown as 0 against a peer median of 0.19, with a published rank of 1. RDY is below the peer median. This may be a positive position, but the local owner should confirm the cohort, denominator, reporting period and whether the national definition matches local waiting list reporting.

Human check: Confirm cohort, denominator, reporting period (Mar-26) and whether local community waiting lists align with the national definition.

Planned surplus/deficit for the financial year.

RDY is shown as 2.39 against a peer median of 0, with a published rank of 1. RDY is above the peer median. This needs finance review before interpretation. The owner should confirm the sign convention, plan version and whether the public figure matches internal board reporting.

Human check: Finance team to confirm sign convention, plan version (2025/26 plan) and whether this matches internal board reporting.

Areas for local review

Urgent Community Response 2-hour performance.

RDY is shown as 79.28 against a peer median of 87.18, with a published rank of 30. RDY is below the peer median. The community urgent care owner should confirm the UCR definition, reporting period and current operational position.

Human check: Community urgent care/service owner to confirm UCR definition, Q4 2025/26 reporting period and local operational position.

Percentage of patients in mental health crisis receiving face-to-face contact within 24 hours.

RDY is shown as 65.54 against a peer median of 71.97, with a published rank of 33. RDY is below the peer median. The crisis pathway owner should confirm the cohort, face-to-face definition and alignment with local crisis standards.

Human check: Crisis pathway owner to confirm crisis cohort, face-to-face definition, Q4 2025/26 period and alignment with local crisis standards.

Percentage of adult mental health inpatients with length of stay over 60 days.

RDY is shown as 46.55 against a peer median of 24.52, with a published rank of 47. RDY is above the peer median. The inpatient mental health lead should confirm the definition, cohort and whether the public figure matches local bed management data.

Human check: Acute/inpatient mental health lead to confirm >60-day definition, Q4 2025/26 cohort and local bed management data.

Relative difference in costs (National Cost Collection Index, adjusted for Market Forces Factor).

RDY is shown as 106.96 against a peer median of 104.85, with a published rank of 36. RDY is above the peer median. For this metric, a value of 100 is approximately expected cost. Finance or productivity leads should confirm the cost index basis, peer group and local productivity narrative.

Human check: Finance/productivity owner to confirm 2024/25 cost index basis, MFF adjustment, peer group and local cost improvement narrative.

Planned surplus/deficit for the financial year.

RDY is shown as 2.39 against a peer median of 0, with a published rank of 1. RDY is above the peer median. This needs finance review before interpretation. The owner should confirm the sign convention, plan version and whether the public figure matches internal board reporting.

Human check: Finance team to confirm sign convention, plan version (2025/26 plan) and whether this matches internal board reporting.

Human check: Finance owner to confirm Month 12 2025/26 YTD basis, plan assumptions and whether amendments have been published.

Relative difference in costs (National Cost Collection Index, adjusted for Market Forces Factor).

Human check: Finance/productivity owner to confirm 2024/25 cost index basis, MFF adjustment, peer group and local cost improvement narrative.

Workforce indicators

Sickness absence rate across the workforce.

RDY is shown as 5.47 against a peer median of 6.22, with a published rank of 13. RDY is below the peer median. The workforce lead should confirm the reporting period, inclusion rules and whether local sickness dashboards use the same definition.

Human check: Workforce/HR lead to confirm reporting period, inclusion rules and whether local sickness dashboards use the same definition.

NHS Staff Survey sub-score on raising concerns.

RDY is shown as 6.85 against a peer median of 6.66, with a published rank of 17. RDY is above the peer median. The OD or workforce lead should confirm survey methodology, response rate, cohort and whether the difference is meaningful for local workforce planning.

Human check: Workforce/OD lead to confirm 2025 Staff Survey methodology, response rate and whether local culture survey data aligns.

NHS Staff Survey engagement theme sub-score.

RDY is shown as 7.11 against a peer median of 7.02, with a published rank of 20. RDY is above the peer median. The OD or workforce lead should confirm survey methodology, response rate, cohort and whether the difference is meaningful for local workforce planning.

Human check: Confirm 2025 survey cohort, theme definition and whether the difference is material in local workforce planning.

Metrics needing cautious interpretation

Annual change in the number of children and young people accessing NHS-funded mental health services.

RDY is shown as 13.37 against a peer median of 8.76, with a published rank of 17. RDY is above the peer median. The CYP mental health lead should confirm numerator, ICB/resident vs provider scope and whether local access data supports the direction of change.

Human check: CYP mental health lead to confirm numerator, ICB/resident vs provider scope and whether local access data supports the direction of change.

Draft interpretation

  • Across Q4 2025/26, 11 raw NOF metrics span 5 domains — several show strong published ranks (e.g. OF0005, OF0079) but need definition checks.
  • The agent flags OF0063, OF0057, OF0016 and OF0086 for local review where RDY sits below peer median on access/long-stay or above on cost index.
  • Finance metrics (OF0079, OF0081, OF0086) need finance-owner interpretation — the agent does not assume surplus/deficit meaning.
  • This snapshot cannot explain why performance changed or what action is needed without local validation.

Human validation checklist

  • Is this the same definition used locally? — Public datasets may use national definitions. Local dashboards may use different filters, denominators or reporting dates.
  • Is this still the latest position? — Public data may be delayed. Local operational data may have moved on since the reporting period shown.
  • Is the figure affected by data quality, suppression, coding or service model? — A public figure can be affected by small numbers, exclusions, coding rules or how services are configured.
  • Who owns the local narrative? — The accountable service, finance, workforce, quality or BI owner should confirm the explanation before use.
  • Is this suitable for board/service reporting, or only a prompt for further review? — These briefs are first drafts — confirm whether the figure is ready for formal reporting or needs more local work.
