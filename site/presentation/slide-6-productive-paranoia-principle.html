Speaker notes

Productive Paranoia is not anxiety — it is designing for the failures you know transitions produce. Complacent experts trust familiar charts; productive paranoia asks “what if meaning changed underneath this refresh?”

Bridge to practice: must-not-fail checks, confidence models, and reconciliation before anyone interprets a variance.

What this slide delivers — plain English

Productive Paranoia
Third operating principle: assume quiet reporting failures during transition and design controls before interpreting variances. B&P withholds or caveats publication when checks fail — even under pressure to “just use the dashboard.”
Prepared vigilance
Must-not-fail lists, confidence tiers, fallback routes and escalation paths agreed in advance — not invented in the meeting when a figure looks wrong.
Novice mindset vs complacency
Experts trust familiar charts; productive paranoia asks “what if meaning changed underneath this refresh?” B&P models that question in assurance routines and senior brief caveats.
Green pipeline ≠ trusted measure
ADF (or any ETL) can succeed while an event type stopped flowing or a field maps to null. B&P owns the publish gate; green technical status is necessary but not sufficient.
