Speaker notes

Walk through three test patterns. Definition migration: when Epic changes how contacts are recorded, “case” and “action” may diverge — test with anchor patients and reconcile before trusting trend lines. MHSDS-like vs local pack: same underlying fact, different inclusion rules — run both in shadow before choosing which goes to the board. Ideas under test: a disciplined register of small experiments with evidence and scale criteria.

Epic and ADF angles: workflow capture changes the events; ADF can be healthy while mappings are wrong. Test mappings, inclusion rules and validation — not just go-live dates.

Keep the quote visible: a new model may not answer the same service question. Offer to show definition-migration vignette if time allows.

What this slide delivers — plain English

Definition migration (case → action)
Worked example: when a new system records “actions” instead of “cases”, headline counts can move without any real change in care. B&P runs anchor-patient reconciles and publishes an agreed figure alongside the dashboard until definitions align.
MHSDS-like submission
A mandatory-style return built to national rules (inclusion flags, reporting period logic). Tests whether Epic/ADF mappings satisfy external definitions — not just local dashboards.
Local demand & capacity pack
A weekly service-facing product from the same underlying facts but with local inclusion rules (e.g. admin contacts excluded). B&P tests both products in parallel before managers rely on either for capacity decisions.
Ideas under test register
A log of small experiments: problem, test, evidence, decision supported and whether to promote, park or dismiss. Shows disciplined improvement portfolio — not every idea becomes a permanent report.
Epic / ADF testing angle
Workflow capture may create or drop event types; ADF may map fields incorrectly while jobs succeed. B&P signs off meaning and inclusion rules; technical teams sign off pipelines.
