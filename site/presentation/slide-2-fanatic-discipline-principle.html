Speaker notes

Fanatic Discipline is about sustaining a performance rhythm while everything around reporting is changing. In a transition, there is pressure to sprint — fix dashboards, answer every query, chase every variance. The discipline is to keep the march: agreed definitions, regular validation, clear owners, reliable handover.

Bridge to the next slide: in practice that means KPI registers, source-to-report maps, and a validation rhythm that survives workflow and pipeline change.

What this slide delivers — plain English

Fanatic Discipline
An operating principle for the secondment: keep performance work on a steady cadence instead of crisis sprints whenever a migration issue appears. For B&P, that means repeatable definitions, checks and handover — not heroic one-off fixes.
10-mile march
Jim Collins’s image for consistent progress at a sustainable pace. Applied here: monthly validation, maintained registers and visible roadmap progress — not burning out the team in month one.
Rhythm not heroics
The deliverable is a system others can run after month six — registers, maps, assurance rhythm — rather than personal expertise locked in one person’s inbox.
Transition context
While Epic changes capture and ADF changes feeds, the B&P partner holds the line on “what we agreed this measure means” so services are not left guessing.
