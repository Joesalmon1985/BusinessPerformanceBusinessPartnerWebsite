Speaker notes

Open by answering the interview question directly: in six months I would focus on transition readiness as the Trust moves toward Epic and Azure Data Factory. Epic changes how clinical and operational events are recorded; ADF changes how data flows into reporting. My contribution as Business & Performance is not to deliver those systems, but to protect performance grip, reporting confidence and service decision-making while they change.

Preview the three principles — disciplined definitions and rhythm, evidence-led testing before embedding, and prepared vigilance for quiet failures. The synthetic DRH demo on this site illustrates types of risk; it is not an Epic replica.

Land the line: the biggest risk is not migration day itself — it is definition migration. Dashboards can refresh while the measure no longer answers the same service question.

What this slide delivers — plain English

Transition readiness
The Trust can still run performance meetings, mandatory returns and service decisions while Epic and ADF are changing — not waiting until go-live to discover figures no longer mean what managers think.
Trusted performance management
Directors and service leads act on agreed figures with known caveats, not on whichever dashboard refreshed last. The B&P role brokers that trust between services, BI and corporate teams.
Epic (in this presentation)
The planned clinical and operational system change — how referrals, contacts and pathways are recorded. I am not proposing to implement Epic; I am proposing to protect what performance means when recording workflows change.
Azure Data Factory (ADF)
The cloud layer that moves data into reporting. A pipeline can run on schedule while field mappings or inclusion rules are wrong. B&P checks meaning and publish rules; IT runs the pipelines.
Performance grip
The ability to explain variances, challenge weak data and keep services focused on the right measures — even when the underlying systems are in flux.
Definition migration
When the rules behind a measure change (e.g. what counts as “referral received”) even though the dashboard label stays the same. Often more dangerous than moving data between servers.
Three principles
Operating habits for the six months: Fanatic Discipline (steady rhythm), Test and Scale (evidence before embedding), Productive Paranoia (controls before judgement). They structure the rest of the presentation.
Synthetic DRH demo on this site
