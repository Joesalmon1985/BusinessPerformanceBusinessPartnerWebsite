Speaker notes

This is where Fanatic Discipline becomes tangible. A KPI register with owners and business rules. A source-to-report map so everyone knows what feeds what. A validation rhythm — not ad hoc firefighting when someone notices a dip.

During Epic transition, workflow capture may change what counts as an event. During ADF rollout, pipelines may refresh on time but map fields differently. The discipline is to agree definitions and checks before and during those changes, and to hand over a pack that the next person can run.

Emphasise: the risk is definition migration, not just data migration. The site’s reporting assurance deliverable merges risk, validation and confidence into one transition-ready artefact.

What this slide delivers — plain English

KPI definitions register
A single maintained list of agreed measures: name, business rule, owner, data source and sign-off status. B&P facilitates agreement with services and holds the register current through Epic/ADF change — the “contract” for what each number means.
Source-to-report map
Lineage from a clinical or operational event (e.g. referral accepted) through warehouse fields to each report and dashboard row. B&P uses it in disputes, impact analysis and handover — “if Epic changes this field, which reports break?”
Reporting requirements map (CSV)
Which reports the organisation must produce, for whom, how often and from which sources. Supports prioritising assurance effort on must-not-fail returns during migration.
Validation rhythm
Scheduled reconciliation and checks — not ad hoc when someone spots a dip. B&P owns the calendar, escalation when meaning drifts and the link to the assurance pack.
Reporting assurance during migration
One combined artefact: risks being watched, validation checks, reconciliation outputs and publish/confidence decisions. Replaces separate risk, validation and confidence documents so sponsors see one story.
Definition migration
The main risk this slide names: measures can change meaning when workflow or pipeline logic changes, even if labels and dashboards look the same. B&P tests and documents that before figures reach the board.
Handover pack / deliverables index
The register of six worked artefacts with owners, status and location — what I would physically leave behind at month six so the next post-holder does not start from scratch.
Reusable approach (ten-step playbook)
A month-by-month method (listen → baseline → define → map → control → validate → prototype → reconcile → embed → handover) reusable for Epic, ADF or any future system change — not a one-off secondment story.

Suggested visual: three-column card layout; callout box for “definition migration”; small Epic + ADF labels on validation card.
