Speaker notes

Close by answering what you would leave behind after six months. Six concrete deliverables: definitions, lineage, merged reporting assurance, demand insight, senior brief, and ideas register. Supporting artefacts: playbook, benefits tracker, handover register, requirements map.

Restate the approach in one breath: disciplined definitions and rhythm through Epic workflow change and ADF pipeline change; evidence-led testing before embedding; prepared vigilance for quiet failures. You are not claiming to deliver Epic or ADF — you are leaving performance grip that survives them.

Offer the deliverables index as the handover pack if they want to explore after the interview. Return to the opening line: transition readiness, not just go-live.

What this slide delivers — plain English

KPI definitions register
Agreed measures with owners and business rules — the reference when Epic changes how events are captured. B&P leaves this maintained, not buried in email.
Source-to-report map
Field-level lineage and report inventory with confidence flags. B&P uses it for impact analysis when ADF mappings or Epic workflows change.
Reporting assurance during migration
Combined risks, validation, reconciliation and publish rules for the transition. The single assurance product sponsors read during Epic/ADF rollout.
Demand & capacity insight
Weekly operational pack (referrals, waits, caseload, agency) tested with service managers — supports capacity and workforce conversations, not just corporate reporting.
Senior performance brief
Reusable monthly template with confidence ratings — how B&P communicates to directors and the Board during unstable data periods.
Ideas under test register
Portfolio of small tests with promote/park/dismiss decisions — shows improvement discipline and what not to scale.
Transition playbook (ten-step change playbook)
Reusable month-by-month method from listen through handover (see reusable approach). Not Epic-specific — a template B&P leaves so the next migration (or phase of Epic/ADF) follows the same discipline. Stored as change_playbook.csv on the site.
Benefits tracker (CSV)
Links improvement work to evidence and target outcomes (e.g. fewer definition disputes, faster publish decisions). B&P uses it to show value beyond “reports delivered” — aligned to corporate benefits realisation.
Handover register
Checklist of every document and deliverable: name, owner, location, last review date, handover status. Ensures month-six outputs survive the post-holder change (handover_documentation_register.csv).
Reporting requirements map
Inventory of what must be reported, to whom and how often — prioritises assurance on mandatory and board-critical products during migration.
Principles & reusable approach
The three habits (Discipline, Test and Scale, Paranoia) plus the ten-step playbook — the “how” behind the six artefacts, so the organisation can repeat the approach without the secondee.

Suggested visual: two-column leave-behind checklist; closing line full-width; link to deliverables index as “handover pack”.
