A proof-of-concept mandatory reporting overview — the kind of visibility a Business & Performance Business Partner would want — demonstrating how agentic AI could help identify, organise and maintain reporting requirements.

How this page was created by agentic AI

This page is a worked example of how a Cursor agent, directed by a human, could draft and maintain a mandatory reporting overview. It was not copied from a live Trust register. The process had three stages.

  1. Searching for information. The agent was asked to identify common NHS mandatory and statutory returns relevant to a mental health and community trust. It searched public NHS England and NHS Digital pages — for example MHSDS, CSDS, RTT, KO41a and DSPT guidance — and cross-checked names against the public source catalogue in public-data/DATA_SOURCE_REGISTER.csv. Where a verified public reference URL existed, it was kept. Returns without a reliable public link were omitted from the demo table.
  2. Deciding how to present it. The agent proposed a layout a Business & Performance Business Partner would actually use: one filterable table showing return name, owner, frequency, next due date, assurance status, risk and issues, with a plain-English glossary for the return codes. The underlying data was stored in data/mandatory_returns_register.csv and synced into this page by R/03_sync_mandatory_register_html.R, so the HTML table stays aligned with the CSV. Filter controls in assets/site.js were added so the register can be searched by owner, risk and assurance status.
  3. Adding fabricated demonstration data. Public specifications do not include local owners, due dates, assurance statuses or internal source-system labels. Those fields were therefore synthetic: plausible team names, demo due dates, illustrative risk ratings and notes such as “coding QA pending” or “needs local confirmation”. Source systems are labelled “(demo)” in the CSV. Confidence levels (confirmed, likely, conditional, needs owner confirmation) flag what would need human validation in a real Trust. A human reviewed the draft, directed changes, and would be the named owner sign-off point before any operational use.

In a live setting, an agent could repeat steps one and two when national specifications change — drafting updates and flagging gaps — but step three must always be completed by named local owners against SOPs and validated systems, not invented by AI.

What "mandatory reporting" means

NHS trusts are required to submit a number of national data returns — standard datasets sent to national bodies on fixed timetables, covering things like waiting times, bed occupancy, complaints and workforce equality. Each return has rules about what to count, who owns it and when it is due. Missing a deadline or submitting poor-quality data carries real consequences, so keeping a reliable grip on every return is core business-partner work.

For a Business & Performance Business Partner, the value is having one clear view of what is due, who owns it, where the risks are, and whether each return is properly assured. The role of an AI agent here is to reduce the admin of keeping that view current — drafting, cross-referencing and flagging changes — not to take over the named owner's responsibility for the figures.

How agentic AI could help

  • Extract reporting requirements from public specifications — for example the Mental Health Services Data Set (MHSDS), Patient Level Contract Monitoring (PLCM) and Aggregate Data Collections (ADC)
  • Propose owner, cadence and source mappings for human confirmation
  • Flag gaps, conflicting cadences and missing documentation links
  • Maintain a change log when national specifications update
  • Cross-reference the public source catalogue in public-data/DATA_SOURCE_REGISTER.csv

What this register maps

  • Return name, owner, frequency and next due date (demo)
  • Assurance status, risk and brief issues / notes
  • Public reference link — verified working URLs only

Additional detail (recipient, mandate, source system, confidence) is held in data/mandatory_returns_register.csv. Returns without a verified public reference are omitted from this demo table for now.

Plain-English glossary of the return codes in this table
MHSDS — Mental Health Services Data Set
The national record of mental health care activity submitted each month.
CSDS — Community Services Data Set
The equivalent national record for community health services for children, young people and adults.
NHS Talking Therapies (formerly IAPT)
Monthly data on talking-therapy services for anxiety and depression, including recovery rates.
ECDS — Emergency Care Data Set
The dataset behind urgent and emergency care (A&E) activity and waiting times.
KH03 — bed availability and occupancy
A return on how many beds are available and how full they are, submitted quarterly.
RTT — Referral to Treatment
Waiting-time data measuring how long patients wait from referral to starting treatment.
DM01 — diagnostic waiting times
Monthly data on waits for diagnostic tests such as audiology and imaging.
CYP eating disorder waits
Children and young people's (CYP) waiting times against the national eating-disorder access standard.
KO41a — written complaints
A return summarising the volume and handling of formal written complaints.
FFT — Friends and Family Test
Patient feedback on whether people would recommend a service.
ERIC — Estates Returns Information Collection
An annual return on buildings, estates and facilities.
DSPT — Data Security and Protection Toolkit
An annual assessment showing the organisation meets data-security standards.
CQC — Care Quality Commission notifications
Alerts the NHS regulator must be told about certain events, often needing clinical sign-off.
WRES / WDES / EDS — workforce equality standards
Annual returns on race and disability equality (WRES, WDES) and the wider Equality Delivery System (EDS).

A view like this brings together assurance, governance, deadline risk and cross-team ownership in one place — the kind of grip on competing priorities and accountability the business-partner role is built around.
